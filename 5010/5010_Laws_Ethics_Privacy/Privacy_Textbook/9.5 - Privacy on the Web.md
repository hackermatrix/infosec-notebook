# 9.5 — Privacy on the Web

> **Core Idea:** The web is the largest arena for privacy threats. Every interaction — browsing, shopping, registering, paying — leaves a trail. Understanding the online environment and its risks is essential for protecting personal information.

---

## 🌐 Understanding the Online Environment

### How Web Tracking Works

Every web interaction generates data that can be collected and analyzed:

| Tracking Method | Description | What It Reveals |
|----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Cookies** | Small text files stored by websites in your browser | Browsing history, preferences, login sessions, shopping carts |
| **Third-party cookies** | Cookies placed by domains other than the one you're visiting (advertisers, trackers) | Cross-site browsing behavior, ad targeting profiles |
| **Web beacons / Tracking pixels** | Invisible 1×1 pixel images embedded in pages or emails | Whether you opened an email, visited a page, your IP address |
| **Browser fingerprinting** | Collecting unique combination of browser settings, plugins, screen resolution, fonts | Identifies you without cookies — extremely difficult to block |
| **IP address logging** | Recording your IP address with each request | Approximate location, ISP, can be linked to identity |
| **Supercookies / Evercookies** | Persistent tracking that regenerates even after cookies are deleted | Persistent identification across sessions |
| **Local storage / IndexedDB** | Browser storage mechanisms with more capacity than cookies | Extended tracking and data persistence |

### The Advertising Ecosystem
- **Ad networks** track users across thousands of websites using third-party cookies
- **Real-Time Bidding (RTB)** — your profile is auctioned to advertisers in milliseconds as a page loads
- **Data Management Platforms (DMPs)** aggregate data from multiple sources to build profiles
- **Retargeting** — ads "follow" you after you visit a product page
- Your browsing history, location, demographics, and interests are all traded as commodities

## 💳 Payments on the Web

### Privacy Risks of Online Payments

| Payment Method | Privacy Level | Risk |
|---------------|--------------|------|
| **Credit/Debit cards** | Low privacy | Transaction data linked to identity; stored by merchants, processors, and banks |
| **PayPal / Digital wallets** | Moderate | Intermediary knows both sides of transaction; merchants get limited info |
| **Prepaid cards** | Higher privacy | Not linked to bank account if purchased with cash |
| **Cryptocurrency (Bitcoin)** | Pseudonymous | Transactions are public on blockchain; identity linkable if exchange account is known |
| **Privacy-focused crypto (Monero)** | High privacy | Transactions are obscured by design |
| **Cash (offline)** | Highest privacy | No digital trail |

### Protecting Payment Privacy
- Use **virtual credit card numbers** (offered by some banks) for online purchases
- Use **intermediary services** (PayPal, Apple Pay) to avoid giving merchants your card number
- **Review statements** regularly for unauthorized charges
- Only transact on **HTTPS** sites (look for the padlock)
- Be wary of sites that ask for **unnecessary information** (SSN for a retail purchase is suspicious)

## 📝 Site and Portal Registrations

### The Data Collection Problem
- Many websites require registration to access content or services
- Registration forms often ask for **more information than necessary**
- This data is stored in databases that can be **breached, sold, or misused**
- Aggregating registrations across sites can build a comprehensive profile

### Strategies for Safer Registration
- **Provide minimum required information** — leave optional fields blank
- **Use a dedicated email address** for registrations (not your primary email)
- **Use pseudonyms** where real identity isn't required
- **Read privacy policies** — especially data sharing and retention practices
- **Use password managers** — unique passwords for every site
- **Disposable email services** for one-time registrations
- **Beware of "Sign in with Google/Facebook"** — convenient but shares data with the identity provider

## 🔍 Whose Page Is This?

### Verifying Website Legitimacy

Not every website is what it claims to be. Privacy risks include:

| Threat | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Phishing sites** | Fake sites that mimic legitimate ones to steal credentials and personal data |
| **Lookalike domains** | URLs that closely resemble real sites (e.g., g00gle.com, paypa1.com) |
| **Fake e-commerce** | Sites that collect payment information but never deliver products |
| **Data harvesting sites** | Sites whose primary purpose is collecting personal information to sell |

### How to Verify
- Check the **URL carefully** — look for misspellings, unusual TLDs
- Verify **HTTPS and certificate details** — click the padlock to inspect
- Look up the site's **registration information** (WHOIS)
- Check for **contact information** — legitimate businesses have verifiable addresses and phone numbers
- Search for **reviews and complaints** from other users
- Be skeptical of **too-good-to-be-true offers**

## 🛡️ Precautions for Web Surfing

### Browser-Level Protections
- **Use private/incognito mode** — doesn't save browsing history, cookies, or form data locally (but doesn't hide traffic from ISP or employer)
- **Clear cookies regularly** or configure browser to block third-party cookies
- **Use ad/tracker blockers** — uBlock Origin, Privacy Badger
- **Enable Do Not Track (DNT)** — though most sites ignore it
- **Use privacy-focused browsers** — Firefox (with strict settings), Brave, Tor Browser
- **Disable JavaScript** selectively for untrusted sites (NoScript)
- **Keep browser updated** — patches close security vulnerabilities

### Network-Level Protections
- **VPN (Virtual Private Network)** — encrypts traffic and masks your IP from websites and your ISP
- **Tor (The Onion Router)** — routes traffic through multiple relays for strong anonymity
- **DNS over HTTPS (DoH)** — encrypts DNS queries so your ISP can't see which sites you visit
- **Avoid public Wi-Fi** for sensitive transactions — or always use VPN

## 🕵️ Spyware

### What Is Spyware?
**Spyware** is software that collects information about a user without their knowledge or consent.

### Types of Spyware

| Type | Behavior |
|------|----------|
| **Keyloggers** | Record every keystroke (capturing passwords, messages, searches) |
| **Screen capture** | Take periodic screenshots of user activity |
| **Browser hijackers** | Change homepage, search engine, redirect traffic |
| **Adware** | Display unwanted advertisements; track browsing for ad targeting |
| **Tracking cookies** | Monitor browsing behavior across sites (gray area — some consider this spyware) |
| **System monitors** | Track system activity — files opened, applications used, sites visited |
| **Stalkerware** | Spyware installed by a partner/family member to monitor someone (a form of abuse) |

### How Spyware Spreads
- **Bundled with free software** — installed alongside legitimate downloads
- **Drive-by downloads** — installed automatically from malicious/compromised websites
- **Email attachments** — malicious attachments that install spyware when opened
- **Fake updates** — "Your Flash Player is out of date!" pop-ups
- **Physical access** — someone installs it directly on your device

### Defending Against Spyware
- **Anti-spyware / antimalware tools** — regular scans
- **Don't install untrusted software** — download only from official sources
- **Keep systems updated** — patch vulnerabilities
- **Be cautious with email attachments** and links
- **Review installed programs** regularly for unfamiliar entries
- **Use a standard (non-admin) user account** for daily activities

## 🛒 Shopping on the Internet

### Privacy Risks of Online Shopping
- Merchants collect **purchase history, browsing behavior, payment information, shipping addresses**
- This data is used for **profiling, targeted marketing, price discrimination**
- **Data breaches** at retailers expose millions of customer records
- **Price discrimination** — showing different prices based on browsing history, location, or device

### Safe Shopping Practices
- Shop on **reputable, well-known sites** with good security track records
- Look for **HTTPS** and verify the site's identity
- Use **credit cards** (better fraud protection) rather than debit cards
- Consider **virtual card numbers** or intermediary payment services
- **Don't save payment information** on merchant sites unless necessary
- Read the **return and refund policy** before purchasing
- **Monitor statements** after purchases for unauthorized charges
- Be cautious with **deals that seem too good to be true**

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

> [!tip] Remember
> - **Cookies** (especially third-party) are the primary web tracking mechanism
> - **Browser fingerprinting** can identify you even without cookies
> - **HTTPS** protects data in transit but doesn't guarantee the site is trustworthy
> - **VPNs and Tor** provide network-level privacy but aren't bulletproof
> - **Spyware** is software that collects data without consent — keyloggers are the most dangerous type
> - **Minimizing data shared** during registration is a simple but effective privacy measure
> - **Incognito mode** hides browsing from the local device only — not from your ISP or the websites
