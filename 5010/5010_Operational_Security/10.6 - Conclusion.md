# 10.6 — Conclusion

> **Core Idea:** Security management is a **continuous, holistic process** — not a one-time project or a purely technical exercise. It requires planning, people, processes, and technology working together, guided by risk analysis and tested through regular exercises.

## 📌 Chapter Summary by Section

### 10.1 — Security Planning
- Organizations need **formal, documented security plans** with executive backing
- Plans must cover policy, assets, controls, roles, and procedures
- A **cross-functional team** creates the plan; **commitment from leadership** makes it work
- The plan is a **living document** requiring regular review

### 10.2 — Business Continuity Planning
- BCP ensures **critical operations survive disruptions**
- Starts with a **Business Impact Analysis (BIA)** to identify what matters most
- Defines **RTO** (how fast to recover) and **RPO** (how much data loss is tolerable)
- Strategies span people, processes, technology, and facilities
- Plans must be **tested** (tabletop → simulation → full test) and **maintained**

### 10.3 — Handling Incidents
- Follow the NIST lifecycle: **Preparation → Detection → Containment/Eradication/Recovery → Lessons Learned**
- **Incident Response Teams** need clear authority, training, and tools
- **Contain first**, investigate second
- **Post-mortem reviews** turn every incident into an improvement opportunity

### 10.4 — Risk Analysis
- **Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact**
- **Quantitative** (ALE = SLE × ARO) provides dollar figures; **Qualitative** (High/Medium/Low) is faster and more accessible
- Four responses: **Mitigate, Transfer, Accept, Avoid**
- Imperfect but essential — still the most rational basis for security decisions

### 10.5 — Dealing with Disaster
- Threats include **natural disasters, power loss, human vandals, and data interception**
- **Physical security** is layered (perimeter → building → interior → equipment)
- **Power protection** (UPS + generators) is non-negotiable for critical systems
- **Contingency planning** with the **3-2-1 backup rule** ensures recovery
- **Encryption** is the primary defense against interception

## 🎓 Key Formulas to Remember

| Formula | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **SLE = Asset Value × Exposure Factor** | Loss from a single incident |
| **ALE = SLE × ARO** | Expected annual loss |
| **Value of Control = ALE(before) − ALE(after) − Cost of Control** | Is the control worth it? |
| **Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact** | Fundamental risk equation |

## 🎯 Key Concepts for Exams

- **BIA** identifies critical business functions and sets RTOs/RPOs
- **Hot site** = ready immediately; **Cold site** = needs days to set up
- **NIST SP 800-61** = the gold standard incident response framework
- **Delphi Method** = anonymous expert consensus for qualitative risk assessment
- **3-2-1 Rule** = 3 copies, 2 media, 1 off-site
- **Gas-based fire suppression** (FM-200) for data centers — water damages equipment
- **Separation of duties** and **least privilege** combat insider threats
- **TEMPEST** = protection against electromagnetic emanation eavesdropping
