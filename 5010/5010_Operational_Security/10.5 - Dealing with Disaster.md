# 10.5 — Dealing with Disaster

> **Core Idea:** Disasters come in many forms — natural, human-made, and technical. Organizations must understand the specific threats they face, implement appropriate physical and environmental safeguards, and have contingency plans ready for when prevention fails.

---

## 🌪️ Natural Disasters

Natural disasters are **unpredictable** in their exact timing but **predictable** in their general likelihood based on geography and history.

### Types of Natural Disasters

| Disaster | Security Impact | Geographic Risk |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Earthquake** | Structural damage, hardware destruction, severed cables, data loss | Fault lines (California, Japan, Turkey, etc.) |
| **Flood** | Water damage to equipment, electrical short circuits, facility inaccessibility | Coastal areas, flood plains, low-lying regions |
| **Hurricane / Typhoon / Cyclone** | Structural damage, prolonged power outages, flooding, facility inaccessibility | Coastal and tropical regions |
| **Tornado** | Sudden, severe structural damage | Central US ("Tornado Alley"), parts of Europe |
| **Fire (Wildfire)** | Complete destruction of facilities and equipment, smoke damage | Dry/forested regions, urban areas |
| **Lightning** | Power surges, equipment damage, fires | Everywhere (but some regions more prone) |
| **Volcanic Eruption** | Ash damage to equipment, facility destruction, air travel disruption | Volcanic regions |
| **Pandemic** | Workforce unavailability, supply chain disruption (not physical infrastructure) | Global |

### Mitigation Strategies for Natural Disasters
- **Geographic risk assessment** — understand what disasters are likely in your area
- **Building standards** — earthquake-resistant construction, flood barriers
- **Geographic separation** — keep backup sites in different risk zones
- **Environmental monitoring** — water sensors, seismic alerts, weather monitoring
- **Insurance** — appropriate coverage for probable natural disasters
- **Redundancy** — distributed data centers and backup facilities

## ⚡ Power Loss

Power loss is one of the **most common** disruptions and can be caused by natural disasters, equipment failure, grid problems, or human error.

### Types of Power Problems

| Problem | Description | Impact |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Blackout** | Complete loss of power | Systems go down immediately |
| **Brownout** | Prolonged reduction in voltage | Equipment malfunction, data corruption |
| **Surge / Spike** | Sudden increase in voltage | Hardware damage, fire risk |
| **Sag / Dip** | Brief reduction in voltage | System resets, data loss |
| **Noise** | Electromagnetic interference in power line | Data corruption, equipment malfunction |
| **Transient** | Very short burst of excess voltage | Component damage over time |

### Power Protection Measures

| Measure | Purpose | Duration |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)** | Battery backup providing immediate power during outage | Minutes to ~1 hour (enough for graceful shutdown or generator startup) |
| **Generator** | Diesel/gas-powered backup electricity | Hours to days (as long as fuel is available) |
| **Surge Protector** | Protects against voltage spikes | Continuous |
| **Power Conditioner / Line Conditioner** | Smooths out voltage fluctuations and noise | Continuous |
| **Redundant Power Feeds** | Dual utility feeds from separate substations | Continuous (protects against single-feed failure) |
| **PDU (Power Distribution Unit)** | Manages and monitors power distribution in data centers | Continuous |

### Best Practices
- Use UPS for **all critical systems** — servers, network equipment, security systems
- UPS should provide enough time to **gracefully shut down** or for the **generator to start**
- Test generators **regularly** (monthly under load)
- Maintain fuel supply agreements for generators
- Monitor power quality continuously
- Implement **automatic failover** between power sources

## 👤 Human Vandals

Human threats include **intentional** malicious acts by insiders or outsiders.

### Types of Human Threats

#### Insiders
- **Disgruntled employees** — sabotage, data theft, or destruction
- **Careless employees** — accidental damage from negligence or ignorance
- **Social engineering victims** — tricked into providing access or information
- **Departing employees** — may take data or leave backdoors
- **Contractors and vendors** — may have access but less loyalty or oversight

#### Outsiders
- **Hackers / Cybercriminals** — unauthorized access for theft, disruption, or ransom
- **Vandals** — physical destruction of property or equipment
- **Activists / Hacktivists** — politically motivated attacks
- **Competitors** — corporate espionage
- **Terrorists** — targeting infrastructure for maximum disruption

### Mitigation Strategies
- **Background checks** — pre-employment screening
- **Principle of Least Privilege** — give people only the access they need
- **Separation of duties** — no single person can complete a critical action alone
- **Access revocation procedures** — immediate termination of all access when an employee leaves
- **Monitoring and auditing** — log and review user activity, especially privileged users
- **Physical security** — access controls, surveillance cameras, security guards
- **Security awareness training** — educate employees about social engineering and insider threats

## 🕵️ Interception of Sensitive Information

Sensitive information can be intercepted through multiple channels.

### Interception Methods

| Method | Description | Countermeasure |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Wiretapping** | Physically tapping network cables or phone lines | Encryption, physical cable protection, fiber optics (harder to tap) |
| **Eavesdropping** | Listening to conversations or monitoring communications | Encryption, secure meeting rooms, awareness training |
| **Shoulder Surfing** | Observing someone's screen or keyboard | Privacy screens, awareness, clean desk policy |
| **Dumpster Diving** | Searching through discarded materials for sensitive information | Shredding, secure disposal, clean desk policy |
| **Emanation / TEMPEST** | Capturing electromagnetic emissions from equipment to reconstruct data | TEMPEST shielding, Faraday cages, distance |
| **Network Sniffing** | Capturing network packets | Encryption (TLS/SSL, VPN), network segmentation, switched networks |
| **Man-in-the-Middle** | Intercepting and potentially modifying communications between two parties | Certificate validation, mutual authentication, encryption |
| **Social Engineering** | Manipulating people into revealing sensitive information | Awareness training, verification procedures |

### Data Protection Principles
- **Encrypt data in transit** — TLS, VPN, SSH, encrypted email
- **Encrypt data at rest** — full disk encryption, database encryption, encrypted backups
- **Classify and label** data by sensitivity
- **Clean desk / Clear screen** policy — sensitive information not left visible
- **Secure disposal** — shred documents, degauss/destroy hard drives, cryptographic erasure
- **Need-to-know access** — restrict access to sensitive information

## 📦 Contingency Planning

Contingency planning is about having **backup plans** ready when primary systems, processes, or facilities fail.

### Core Elements

#### Data Backup and Recovery
| Backup Type | Description | Recovery Speed | Storage |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|---------|
| **Full Backup** | Complete copy of all data | Fastest restore | Most storage |
| **Incremental** | Only data changed since the last backup (any type) | Slower (needs full + all incrementals) | Least storage |
| **Differential** | Only data changed since the last full backup | Medium (needs full + latest differential) | Moderate storage |
| **Mirror / Snapshot** | Real-time copy of data | Instant failover | Same as full |

##### The 3-2-1 Rule
- **3** copies of your data
- **2** different storage media/types
- **1** copy stored off-site (geographically separate)

#### Alternate Processing Sites
(See [[10.2 - Business Continuity Planning]] for Hot/Warm/Cold site details)

#### Communication Contingencies
- Redundant communication channels (phone, email, messaging apps, satellite phone)
- Out-of-band communication — if primary systems are compromised, use independent channels
- Emergency notification systems — mass alerts to all personnel

#### Supply Chain Contingencies
- Multiple vendors for critical supplies
- Emergency procurement procedures
- Inventory buffers for essential items

## 🏗️ Physical Security

Physical security is the **first line of defense**. If an attacker has physical access, most technical controls can be bypassed.

### Layers of Physical Security (Defense in Depth)

```
OUTER PERIMETER
  ├── Fencing, walls, gates
  ├── Lighting (especially at entry points)
  ├── Surveillance cameras (CCTV)
  └── Security guards / patrols

BUILDING PERIMETER
  ├── Locked doors and windows
  ├── Access control systems (badge readers, biometrics)
  ├── Mantrap / Airlock entries
  ├── Visitor management (sign-in, escorts, badges)
  └── Intrusion detection (alarms, motion sensors)

INTERIOR
  ├── Locked server rooms / data centers
  ├── Badge access to restricted areas
  ├── Surveillance cameras in sensitive areas
  └── Environmental controls (see below)

EQUIPMENT / DATA
  ├── Cable locks for laptops
  ├── Locked cabinets for media
  ├── Encryption for portable devices
  └── Secure disposal procedures
```

### Environmental Controls

| Control | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **HVAC** | Maintain proper temperature and humidity for equipment (18-27°C / 64-80°F, 40-60% humidity) |
| **Fire Detection** | Smoke detectors, heat detectors, flame detectors |
| **Fire Suppression** | Sprinklers (wet/dry pipe), gas-based systems (FM-200, Inergen, CO2) for data centers |
| **Water Detection** | Moisture sensors under raised floors and near pipes |
| **EMI Shielding** | Protect against electromagnetic interference |

#### Fire Suppression for Data Centers
- **Water-based systems** damage electronics — avoid in server rooms if possible
- **Gas-based systems** (FM-200, Novec 1230, Inergen) suppress fire without damaging equipment
- Gas systems require **sealed rooms** and **safety warnings** (some displace oxygen)
- Must have both **automatic** and **manual** activation options
- Integrate with HVAC to shut down airflow during suppression

## 🔄 Recap

The complete disaster preparedness picture:

| Threat Category | Key Examples | Primary Defenses |
|----------------|--------------|------------------|
| **Natural Disasters** | Earthquake, flood, fire, hurricane | Geographic separation, environmental monitoring, building standards, insurance |
| **Power Loss** | Blackout, surge, brownout | UPS, generators, redundant feeds, surge protection |
| **Human Vandals** | Insiders, hackers, terrorists | Access control, monitoring, least privilege, background checks |
| **Interception** | Wiretapping, sniffing, social engineering | Encryption, physical security, training, clean desk policy |
| **Facility Loss** | Any of the above | Contingency planning, backup sites, 3-2-1 backups |

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

> [!tip] Remember
> - **Physical security** is the foundation — all other security depends on it
> - **Power protection** (UPS + generator) is essential for every critical facility
> - **Defense in depth** — multiple layers of physical and environmental controls
> - **Insider threats** are often more dangerous than outsiders because they already have access
> - **Encryption** is the primary defense against data interception
> - **3-2-1 backup rule** — 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 off-site
> - **Contingency planning** assumes failure will happen and prepares alternatives
