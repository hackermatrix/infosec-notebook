**Record** = the proof (unchangeable evidence that rules were followed)

During an ISO 27001 audit, the auditor reviews your **documents** to understand what you said you'd do, checks the **specifications** to confirm they meet the standard's requirements, and then asks for **records** to prove you actually did it. If you have a policy saying "access reviews must be done quarterly" (document) but no evidence of quarterly access reviews (record) — that's a non-conformity.

This is the same evidence collection work you did at Deloitte — gathering audit artifacts is essentially gathering records that prove controls are operating.![[Pasted image 20260308230404.png]]
