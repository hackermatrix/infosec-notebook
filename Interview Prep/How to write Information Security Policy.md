**Scope of the policy extended to cloud environments**. This is critical because if an organization's data encryption policy only says "all data at rest must be encrypted with AES-256" without mentioning cloud, someone could argue that S3 buckets or RDS instances aren't covered since they're not "our" infrastructure — they're AWS's. By explicitly stating "data at rest in cloud environments, on-premises systems, and third-party hosted environments," the policy closes that loophole and makes it clear that the encryption standard applies regardless of where the data lives.
