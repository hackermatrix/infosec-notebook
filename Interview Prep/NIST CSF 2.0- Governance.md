You have a data-center, and everything is on-prem, now you have decided to move everything in the cloud like AWS, AWS is the third party here. 

- 1. **Identify** — What data classify the data, is it senstive client data, cardholfer data, systems, classifications
- 2. **Compliance Identification** — What regulations, standards, and contractual obligations apply to this data, e.g under GDPR, PCI 
- 3. **Review Vendor and Client Contractual Obligations**: do they mandate data residency in specific regions? Is multi-tenancy allowed or do they require dedicated infrastructure, do they want to be custodian of the key? 
- 4. **Shared Responsibility Model** — Now that you know what you have AND what rules apply, define who owns what between you and AWS
- 5.**Assess Risks** — What threats and vulnerabilities exist given the above
- 6. **Evaluate Security Controls** — Are existing controls sufficient for the cloud environment
- 7. **Compliance Impact Assessment** — How does all of the above change our existing compliance posture (PCI scope, ISO ISMS, SOC 2 report)
-- SOC 2 controls may change
- ISO ISMS needs cloud risk register updates
- 8. **Update Policies** — Formalize everything into updated or new policies
- 9. **Ongoing Monitoring** — Implement continuous monitoring post-migration
